Dissection

Market Information

The Fisher Price: Laugh & Learn: Lil' Gamer was released in 2021. It is a toy intended to entertain and educate children from ages 6 months to 36 months. With its game cartridge, directional pad, and A and B buttons, the product is clearly meant to imitate handheld game systems like the Nintendo Game Boy from 30 years prior in 1989. The Game Boy was a rectangular handheld device with cartridges that could be inserted to play different games, in a similar form-factor to the Lil' Gamer. This form-factor attracts adults that may recognize the Game Boy while offering a stimulating and educational form of entertainment for the children that adults would be buying toys for. This likely improves Mattel's sales figures, as the Lil' Gamer is the first children's toy to directly imitate the Game Boy.

The official Mattel website listing for the Lil' Gamer advertises its design as teaching letters, numbers, directions, and shapes. The same listing points to the physical interactions available on the toy as benefiting dexterity and hand-eye coordination. Most retail outlets sell the Lil' Gamer at a base price of $12.99, so this is presumably close to the MSRP value. Larger outlets like Amazon and Walmart likely reduce the price for some products to beat their competitors.

As this is a one-of-a-kind children's toy and it was only released three years ago, there are not many imitation or rebranded versions, but the one that does exist is a pink version pictured below, which according to Amazon was released in 2020 prior to the blue version, but is harder to find at retail outlets. Besides the pink plastic case, other differences include a female dog on the screen and game cartridge, the purple rather than blue coloring of the directional pad numbers, and the purple rather than red coloring of the bottom slidable block on the edge.
